Why I'm Taking a Break From School in 2022

2022-02-12
1 minute read
academics

Disclaimer: The title of this post is somewhat misleading, as I am still technically enrolled as a full-time student, but I am taking the absolute bare minimum amount to remain eligible for my scholarships that require me to stay a full-time student (I still don’t even understand the rules given I’d be saving the university money by not taking classes, but I digress). By no means am I saying that everyone should do this, however I think it’s the best decision for some people under some super specific conditions.

Ever since the latter half of high school, I’ve been trying to optimize my class schedules to the best of my capabilities. I took as many AP and dual-enrollment courses as I could in high school to maximize the amount of credit I’d receive, and I packed every semester to the brim for every semester of college. While I wasn’t trying to graduate early, I just had this feeling that I needed to be spending my time in the most efficient way possible. That led me to taking some absolutely brutal class schedules that have only left me feeling burnt out.
